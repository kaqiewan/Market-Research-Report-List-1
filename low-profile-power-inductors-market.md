<p><ul><li>Unshielded</li><li>Shielded</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The low profile power inductors market is divided into two main types: unshielded and shielded inductors. Unshielded inductors are typically more compact and cost-effective but can emit electromagnetic interference (EMI), making them suitable for low-frequency applications. In contrast, shielded inductors feature a casing that minimizes EMI, making them ideal for noise-sensitive environments and high-frequency applications. The choice between these types depends on factors like space constraints, performance requirements, and sensitivity to interference in electronic designs.</p></p>
