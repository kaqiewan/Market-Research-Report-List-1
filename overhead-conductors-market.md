<p><p>The overhead conductors market is primarily categorized into two types: conventional conductors and high-temperature conductors. Conventional conductors, often made from aluminum or copper, are widely used due to their cost-effectiveness and established reliability. They function efficiently at standard operating temperatures. In contrast, high-temperature conductors are designed to operate at elevated temperatures without thermal degradation, allowing for higher current capacity and reduced line losses. These advanced conductors support enhanced power transmission efficiency and are increasingly favored in modern electrical infrastructure.</p></p>
