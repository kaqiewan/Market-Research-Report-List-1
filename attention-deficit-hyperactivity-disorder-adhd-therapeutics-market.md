<p><p>The ADHD therapeutics market is primarily divided into two categories: stimulants and non-stimulants. Stimulants, such as methylphenidate and amphetamines, enhance dopamine and norepinephrine neurotransmission, effectively improving attention and reducing hyperactive behaviors. Non-stimulants, like atomoxetine and guanfacine, offer alternative options for those who may not tolerate stimulants or prefer a different mechanism of action, focusing on norepinephrine reuptake inhibition or alpha-2 adrenergic receptor modulation. Both types aim to manage ADHD symptoms and improve patients' daily functioning.</p></p>
