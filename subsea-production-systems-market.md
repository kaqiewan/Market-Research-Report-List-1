<p><p>Subsea production systems are categorized into two main market types: stationary and floating. Stationary systems are fixed installations on the seabed, commonly utilized in shallow waters, offering stability and ease of maintenance. In contrast, floating systems are designed to operate in deeper waters, leveraging floating platforms that can move with ocean currents and waves. Both types serve the oil and gas industry by enabling efficient extraction and processing of subsea resources while addressing varying environmental and logistical challenges.</p></p>
