<p><p>The albumin market comprises three primary types: Human Serum Albumin (HSA), Bovine Serum Albumin (BSA), and Recombinant Albumin. HSA is derived from human plasma and is widely used in therapies and diagnostics. BSA, sourced from bovine blood, is commonly utilized in research and laboratory applications. Recombinant Albumin, produced through genetic engineering, offers a safer alternative free from animal-derived impurities and is gaining traction in therapeutics and biotechnology. Each type serves distinct roles in healthcare and research sectors.</p></p>
