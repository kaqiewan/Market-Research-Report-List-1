<p><p>The food humectants market is categorized into natural and synthetic types. Natural humectants, derived from plant sources like honey and glycerin, are favored for their health benefits and clean labels. They retain moisture and enhance texture in food products. Conversely, synthetic humectants are chemically produced and often offer cost advantages and consistent performance. These include substances like propylene glycol and sorbitol, which effectively prevent dehydration and extend shelf life, catering to diverse food processing requirements.</p></p>
