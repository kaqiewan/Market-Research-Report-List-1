<p><p>The pharmaceutical emulsifiers market is divided into two main types: natural and synthetic emulsifiers. Natural emulsifiers, derived from plant or animal sources, are favored for their biocompatibility and lower side effects, making them ideal for drug formulations. Examples include lecithin and gum acacia. Conversely, synthetic emulsifiers are chemically manufactured and offer advantages like consistency and stability, often used in formulations requiring precise control over properties. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing the stability and delivery of pharmaceutical products.</p></p>
